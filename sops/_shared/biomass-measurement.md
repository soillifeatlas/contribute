# Biomass measurement

Wet mass is required on every submission. Dry mass (freeze-dried) is optional but strongly preferred because the atlas quantification pipeline (internal-standard normalisation + response-ionisation-efficiency correction) depends on dry-weight units.

## Equipment

- Analytical balance (± 0.1 mg) for biomass <100 mg; standard balance for larger
- Freeze-drier (lyophiliser) with vacuum ≤ 0.1 mbar and shelf temperature ≤ −40 °C
- Glass or ceramic weigh-boats (pre-weighed, tare recorded)

## Protocol

1. Weigh the empty, dry weigh-boat; record mass.
2. Transfer biomass to weigh-boat; record wet mass (wet_mg = total − boat).
3. Transfer to freeze-drier; lyophilise to constant mass (typically ≥ 24 h; verify by weighing at 12-h intervals).
4. Record dry mass (dry_mg = total − boat).
5. Store freeze-dried biomass in sealed container with desiccant.

## Reporting

Report wet_mg always. Report dry_mg when measured. Do not interpolate or estimate dry mass from wet mass — leave the field blank in the form.
